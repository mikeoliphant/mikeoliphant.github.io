Sun God Chicken


This recipe was an attempt to recreate a dish that Mike liked at the UCSD food court back in the early 90's.

Updated 11/15/08
We are in the process of modifying this recipe. Still had too much sauce, but proportions were improved (and you may need the extra sauce if doing the deep fry).

I need to check -- Keep note of vinegar acidity: I used a mix of "adjusted to 4.3%" and "above 4.5%" (higher is nicer I think).

Use more onion than bell pepper.


For the cooking sauce:

3 tablespoons sugar
1/3 cup rice wine vinegar
2 tablespoons dark soy sauce
1/3 cup unsalted chicken stock
1 tablespoon cornstarch
1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
1/2 teaspoon chili paste (sambal oelek)
1/2 teaspoon hot red chile flakes


For the stir-fry:

3-4 boneless skinless chicken thighs or 1 breast, cut into 1/2 to 1-inch pieces (about 12 ounces)
2 tablespoons canola oil
2 garlic cloves, minced
2/3 red bell pepper, cut into strips
1 1/2 onions, cut into strips
10 green onions, cut into 1-inch pieces, white and green parts separated


Option 1: Coat the chicken pieces in the batter and deep fry until golden. Set aside in a warm place.
Option 2: Coat the chicken pieces in the marinade and let sit 15 minutes.

Heat a wok over high heat; when hot, add the oil and garlic. Stir-fry the chicken pieces for 2 minutes (for Option 2), then add bell pepper strips and cook an additional 2 minutes. Add onion strips, and the whites of the green onion. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes or until the vegetables are crisp tender. (Add deep fried chicken now for Option 1).

Pour the cooking sauce over the stir-fry, mix well and cook about 60 seconds to thicken. Remove from heat and stir in the green parts of the green onion.

Serve over hot cooked short grained rice and garnish with extra green onion slivers.

For the batter (Option 1):

1 egg
1/3 cup flour
1/4 teaspoon salt
1 tablespoon water


For the marinade (Option 2):

1 small garlic clove, minced
1 teaspoon rice wine vinegar
2 teaspoons soy sauce
1/4 teaspoon hot red chile flakes
1/2 teaspoon canola oil
