Basque Chicken


This recipe was found in a French cookbook borrowed from the library while living in Edinburgh. We've modified it to use skinless chicken since the skin would often become mushy during the cooking process.

1-2 tablespoons olive oil
6 ounces ham or bacon, diced
8 chicken pieces, skinned (2 legs, 2 thighs, breasts cut in half)
3-4 onions, sliced long
3 garlic cloves, crushed
2 large green peppers, sliced long
1/4 teaspoon marjoram
14 ounces canned tomatoes
1/2 cup chicken stock
Kosher salt
Freshly ground black pepper
2 tablespoons chopped parsley


Saute bacon in 1 T olive oil, or ham in 2 T olive oil, until lightly browned. Remove and keep warm.

Cook chicken in same oil until uniformly browned. Remove and keep warm.

Cook onions and garlic in same oil until soft and golden. Add peppers, marjoram and a sprinkle of salt, then cover and cook gently for 10 minutes.

Add tomatoes, stock, salt and pepper. Return the chicken and bacon to the pan. Cover and cook gently 40-45 minutes or until chicken is cooked and tender.

Serve over warm basmati rice.
