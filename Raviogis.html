Raviogis


Makes about 24 large raviogi. Serve 5-6 raviogi per person.

Also successfully prepared with a mixture of sharp cheddar and gruyere cheeses.
Used about 2 1/2 ounces each of onion and shallot; 2-3 ounces of cheese.

1 1/2 cups sauce on 9/11/09

For the Raviogi Filling:

2-3 (1 pound) potatoes
2 shallots, minced
1/4 onion, cut into 1/4-inch dice
1 small garlic clove, minced
3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, divided
3/4 cup grated mild cheddar cheese
1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
Freshly ground black pepper
1 tablespoon minced fresh marjoram (optional)


For the Pasta Dough:

300 grams flour
3 eggs
3/4 teaspoon extra virgin olive oil


For the Marjoram Marinara:

Mario Sauce (basic tomato sauce)
1 tablespoon chopped fresh marjoram


Boil the potatoes in their skins until tender, about 25 minutes. Remove from water and let cool about 10 minutes.

Meanwhile, on medium heat, saute the shallots, onion, and garlic in 1 tablespoon of the olive oil. Cook until the onion is soft and translucent, but not brown.

Peel the potatoes and place in a medium bowl. Using a fork, mash until fairly smooth. Add the remaining 2 tablespoons olive oil, the sauted onion mixture, the cheese, salt, pepper and marjoram and mix thoroughly.

Use about 1 1/2 teaspoon of the potato mixture for each raviogi.

After cooking in boiling salted water, lightly sauce with warm Marjoram Marinara and serve.
