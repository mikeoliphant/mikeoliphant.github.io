Corn Chowder with Smoked Andouille


If your sausage is nicely smokey and your corn is very fresh and it'll turn out great. Nice with open-faced grilled cheese sandwiches (cave-aged gruyere on toasted ciabatta) along side.

1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
1/2 small onion, finely diced
1 celery stalk, finely diced
1 cup milk
1 cup chicken stock
2-3 ears corn
5 oz andouille sausage


Heat the olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium-low heat. Add the onion and celery; let sweat until just translucent.

Cut the kernals off the corn cobs. You should have about 2 1/2 cups of corn kernals. Break the cobs in half and use the back of a knife to scrap the corn juices from cobs into the sauce pan, then add the cobs to the pan.

Add the milk and chicken broth to the pan and bring to a low simmer. Add a couple pinches of salt. Simmer about 15 minutes. Remove and discard the corn cobs.

Add 1 1/2 cups of the corn kernals to the pan, then use an immersion blender to puree the soup. Cut the andouille into quarters lengthwise, then cut into 1/4-inch pieces. Add the sausage to the pan along with the remaining 1 cup corn. Simmer gently for about 5 minutes. Taste salt, adjust then serve.
