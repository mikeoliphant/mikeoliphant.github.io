Smoked Duck


Great served with cheesy polenta.

1 duck (~5 lb)
1 quart apple juice
2 quarts water
3/4 cup (190 grams) kosher salt
3 tablespoons (36 grams) sugar
2 garlic cloves, smashed
Several small, sprigs thyme
1/2 teaspoon cracked black peppercorns


Mix brine ingredients together and stir to dissolve the salt and sugar. Chill thoroughly.
Remove the head, neck and feet from the duck and rinse. Score the skin and trim off excess fat. Submerge the duck in the brine and refrigerate at least 24 hours or up to 48. Remove from brine and place on a rack. Refrigerate uncovered for about 24-36 hours.
Let rest at room temperature 45-60 minutes before cooking. Pick skin all over with a toothpick.
Smoke using alder and pecan woods at 210-220 degrees (full water pan) for about 4 1/2 hours. Final duck internal temperature should be 165-170 degrees.
