Khao Kha Moo (Thai Braised Pork Leg on Rice)


Serve with hot jasmine rice, quick pickled gai choi (chinese mustard greens), and thai boiled eggs (boil water, add eggs, cook for 7-8 minutes, remove eggs and immediately cool in ice water).
Season at the table with the Vinegar Chili Dip.


GG Paste:

15 grams cilantro stem, finely chopped
4 (12 gram) garlic cloves
3/4 teaspoon white peppercorns
20 grams galangal


Braise:

2 tablespoons oil, divided
2 1/2 pounds pork leg, skin-on
1 pound pork shoulder
2 small star anise
1 (2 inch) piece cinnamon stick
20 grams palm sugar
4-5 tablespoons soy sauce
2 tablespoons dark soy sauce
2 liters water


Vinegar Chili Sauce:

1 ounce jalapenos, minced
1-2 hot green chilies (thai or serrano)
4 grams cilantro stem, minced
4 (12 gram) garlic cloves
1/2 cup white vinegar
1 tablespoon sugar


Prepare the GG Paste: put everything except the oil into a mortar and pound to a nice paste.

Braise: Put 1 tablespoon of oil into a large pot and heat on medium high. Lightly brown the skin side of the pork, then remove and set aside.
Put the remaining table spoon of oil into the pot and add the GG paste, star anise, and cinnamon. Saute about a minute, or until fragrant. Remove and set aside. Place all of the pork into the pot and add 2 liters of water. Bring to a boil and simmer about 15 minutes skimming off any foam and debris. Add the palm sugar, sauted GG paste, spices and soy sauces to the pot. Simmer uncovered about 30 minutes (lvl 2.8), then partially cover and simmer another couple of hours (lvl 2.4-6) or until meat and skin are both tender. Taste the broth; if necessary, remove the meat and allow it to reduce somewhat. Adjust with soy sauce or palm sugar if desired. Return the meat to the pot to rewarm. Slice some meat with and without skin and serve over jasmine rice along with a generous ladle of the broth.

Prepare the Vinegar Chili Sauce: Put the chilies, cilantro stems, salt and garlic into a mortar and pound to a nice paste. Transfer to a jar, and add the vinegar and sugar. Shake until sugar is dissolved. Use for seasoning everthing at the table.
