Jambalya


This version of jambalya is drier than most and uses long grained rice rather than short. It's fairly quick to prepare.

Rice:

1 cup long grained rice
Chicken broth
1/2 onion, chopped
1 garlic clove, minced
1 celery stalk, diced
Olive oil
1/2 teaspoon kosher salt


Mix-Ins:

1/2 onion, large dice
1 celery stalk, sliced
1/4 red bell pepper, diced
1/4 green bell pepper, diced
Olive oil
1/2 boneless skinless chicken breast, sliced thinly and across
6 ounces spicy cajun style sausage, cut in half lengthwise and sliced (andouille or other)
Approx. 1 (14 ounce) can kidney beans, rinsed
6 ounces shrimp, peeled and cleaned (optional)
4 ounces sea scallops, quartered (optional)
1 (14 ounce) can whole plum tomatoes, juice reserved and tomatoes diced
2 tablespoons chopped celery leaves (optional)


Spices:

3/4 teaspoon dried basil
3/4 teaspoon dried oregano
1/2 teaspoon dried thyme sprigs
1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
1/4-1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
2 teaspoons paprika


Saute the onion, celery and garlic in a little olive oil. Rinse the rice three times, then cook in chicken broth with the sauted vegetables and 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt.

While the rice is cooking, saute the remaining onion, celery, red and green bell peppers until softened. If using, add the shrimp and cook for about two minutes or until opaque. Turn off the heat, add the spice mixture and stir through. In a separate pan, quickly sear the sausage and cook the chicken strips and scallops (if using). Add to the onion, celery, pepper mixture.

When the rice is done, gently stir in the spiced vegetable meat mixture, the tomatoes, and kidney beans. Mix in a little reserved tomato juice if desired. Add salt to taste and keep warm for 10 minutes while the flavors meld. Add the celery leaves just before serving if using. Lightly fluff and stir, then serve.
