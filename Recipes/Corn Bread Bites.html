Corn Bread Bites


Original recipe used cream-style corn (8 3/4 ounce can) and no green chiles.

Makes 36. Serving size: 3 muffins.

2/3 cup flour
1/2 cup cornmeal
1 tablespoon sugar
1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
1/4 teaspoon salt
1 (14 ounce) can corn, drained and liquid reserved
1/2 cup (2 ounces) shredded sharp cheddar cheese
1/2 cup reduced-fat sour cream
1/4 cup thinly sliced green onions
Dash Hot Sauce
1 large egg, lightly beaten
2 canned green chiles, rinsed and cut into 1/4-inch dice
Cooking spray


Preheat oven to 375 degrees.

Combine flour, cornmeal, sugar, baking powder and salt in a large bowl.

Put corn into a food processor along with about 1/4 cup of the reserved liquid and pulse 4 to 5 times to roughly chop the corn. Mix the corn puree with the cheese, sour cream, green onions, hot sauce, egg and chiles. Gently stir the corn mixture into the flour mixture until just combined.

Lightly spray 3 miniature muffin tins with cooking oil or spray. Divide the batter evenly into 36 wells (a very small ice-cream scoop works well).

Bake for 10 to 12 minutes or until lightly browned. Cool 2 minutes in pans, then loosen the edges with a knife and remove muffins from pans. Best served warm.
