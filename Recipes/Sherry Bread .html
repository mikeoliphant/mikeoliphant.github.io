Sherry Bread


We use two variants of a single recipe for making flat bread to have with curry and tortillas for having with Mexican food.

2 cups flour
1 teaspoon salt
1/2 cup water, lukewarm
1/4 cup evaporated milk, room temperature


for flat-bread --:

1 teaspoon baking powder
1 1/2 teaspoons canola oil


for tortillas --:

1/4 teaspoon baking powder
3 teaspoons canola oil


In a large bowl mix flour, salt and baking powder together. Add oil to flour mixture and mix with your fingertips to combine.

Add water and milk, mixing with your hands to form a soft dough. Knead on a lightly floured surface for 1 minute. Dough should be “earlobe’’ soft and not sticky.
Cover with a damp cloth and let rest for 30 minutes.

Divide dough into 8 or 12 balls (depending on how big you want them to be), cover, and let rest another 15 minutes.

Heat a heavy griddle or skillet (dry) over med/high heat for 5 minutes.

Roll each ball into a disk – as thin as possible for tortillas, and about ¼ inch thick for flat-bread.

Cook each bread about 30 seconds on each side, or until it looks dry and slightly wrinkled with a few brown specks.

Place each finished bread into a plastic bag while you finish the cooking remaining dough – this helps them stay soft until time to serve.

Serve warm, wrapped in a dish towel. Leftovers can be stored in the fridge for several days, and then briefly microwaved in a loose plastic bag.
