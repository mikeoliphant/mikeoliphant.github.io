Cochinita Pibil


Traditionally, sour oranges are used as the citrus component. If habaneros aren't available, 3-4 red serrano chiles (with seeds) can be substituted.

Annatto seeds are very hard and cannot be ground in a mortor and pestle. A coffee grinder dedicated to grinding spices works well.

See Smoked Version at bottom of page.

2 1/2 tablespoons annatto seeds
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
2 teaspoons mexican oregano
1 1/2 teaspoons black peppercorns
5 whole allspice berries
5 whole cloves
1 (2-3 inch) stick cinnamon
1 habanero pepper, seeded
1/2 cup orange juice
1/3 cup lime juice
3 tablespoons lemon juice
1 tablespoon salt
4 garlic cloves
Dash tequilla
3 pounds boneless pork butt, cut in 2-inch squares
Banana leaves


Grind the annatto seeds to a fine powder using a spice grinder. Repeat with the cumin seeds, oregano, peppercorns, allspice, cinnamon and cloves. Sieve with a fine mesh strainer to remove grit.

Place the liquids, salt, garlic, habanero and powdered spices into a blender and blend well. Combine the marinade with pork chunks in a large ziplock bag. Refrigerate at least two hours or overnight.

Line a baking dish with banana leaves that have been softened over a flame or hot burner. Pour the pork and marinade into the dish and wrap with the leaves. Cover the pan with foil and roast in a preheated, 325 degree oven for 4 hours. Remove from oven and let rest 20 minutes. Serve with plain rice and a simple green salad.

Less juicy marinade for Smoked version:

2 1/2 tablespoons annatto seeds
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
2 teaspoons mexican oregano
1 1/2 teaspoons black peppercorns
5 whole allspice berries
5 whole cloves
1 (2-3 inch) stick cinnamon
1 habanero pepper, seeded OR 3 T habanero hot sauce
3 tablespoons orange juice
2 tablespoons lime juice
1 tablespoon lemon juice
1 tablespoon salt
4 garlic cloves
1 tablespoon tequilla
3 pounds boneless pork butt, cut in 2-4 inch squares
Banana leaves


Smoked cook method:

Grind the annatto seeds to a fine powder using a spice grinder. Repeat with the cumin seeds, oregano, peppercorns, allspice, cinnamon and cloves. Sieve everything with a fine mesh strainer to remove grit.

Place the liquids, salt, garlic, habanero and powdered spices into a blender and blend well. Combine the marinade with pork chunks in a large ziplock bag. Refrigerate at least two hours or overnight.

Line a large tray with two or three layers of banana leaves that have been softened over a flame or hot burner. Put the pork and marinade into the center and fully wrap with the leaves. Carefully tie with a string to keep packet closed. Move to a small, shallow, foil-wrapped tray.

Prepare smoker with full water pan, full charcoal and several small chunks of pecan and hickory smoke wood. Place tray and packet into smoker on top rack. Maintain heat at 260-270 degrees and cook about 6 hours. Remove from smoker and let rest 30 minutes.

Serve as tostadas topped with shredded pork, avocado, tomato, pickled onion, shredded lettuce and habanero hot sauce. (Crumbled cotija or feta would be a nice accent.) Accompany with small bowls of saucy black beans.
