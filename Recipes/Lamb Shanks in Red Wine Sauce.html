Lamb Shanks in Red Wine Sauce




3 small lamb shanks (about 12 oz each)
2 tablespoons oil
1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt, divided
3 carrots, peeled
5 celery stalks
1 large onion
5 garlic cloves
2 teaspoons thyme
2 tablespoons tomato paste
2 tablespoons AP flour
2 bay leaves
2 cups red wine
2 cups stock
8 ounces small white mushrooms
Butter


Season each shank with 1/4 t salt. Heat a small dutch oven and add the oil. Brown the shanks on all sides; remove from pan. Cut the carrots, celery and onions into 1 inch pieces. Slice the garlic cloves. Add the vegetables to the pan and lightly brown them. Stir in the tomato paste and thyme and cook another minute. Stir in the flour and cook one minute longer.
Return the shanks to the pan. Add the wine, 3/4 t salt and bay leaves and bring to a boil; simmer about 10 minutes. Add the stock and return to a boil.
Cover and place in a pre-heated 375 degree oven for 2 hours. Meanwhile, gently cook the mushrooms in butter on the stove top. Remove the dutch oven from the oven and add the mushrooms. Return to the oven, uncovered for another 30 minutes or until shanks are tender.
Remove from the oven. Transfer some of the stock to a small sauce pan and reduce until slightly thickened and glossy. Return the thickened sauce to the pot and let everything rest for at least 15 minutes. Serve over polenta or mashed potatoes (one shank per person, plus some sauce and vegetables).
