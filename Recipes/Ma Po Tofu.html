Ma Po Tofu


Needed more chile paste and salt. Could've used a splash of vinegar. Mike wasn't sure he liked the sichuan peppercorns.

Firm Tofu (1/2 twin pack)
4 ounces ground beef
Canola oil
1 teaspoon sichuan peppercorns, lightly crushed
1/2 t hot pepper flakes
1 tablespoon chile bean sauce
1 tablespoon soy sauce
1 tablespoon black bean sauce
1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
2 tablespoons corn starch
1/c c chicken broth (unsalted)
2 garlic cloves, minced
2 teaspoons minced ginger
2 green onions, chopped
Sesame oil, for garnish
Chopped cilantro, for garnish


Dry tofu and cut it into 1/2 cubes. In a small bowl, mix together the chile bean sauce, soy sauce, black bean sauce and salt. Stir the corn starch into the chicken broth, set aside.

Toast sichuan peppercorns and chile flakes in a little hot oil for a few seconds - until fragrant. Add garlic, ginger and beef. Stir until cooked through, then mix in the sauce mixture.

When hot, add the corn starch-broth mixture and the tofu cubes. Stir gently, breaking up a few tofu cubes, but keeping most whole. Simmer about a minute to thicken the sauce. Serve over rice, garnished with the green onions, cilantro and a few drops of sesame oil.
