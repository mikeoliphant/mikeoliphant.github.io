Kung Pao Chicken


Keeping the size of the chicken and scallions similar to the size of the peanuts makes for a dish that is pleasing to eat. Use a spoon rather than chopsticks, but have chopsticks handy for eating a side of asian vegetables such as gai lan or bok choy.

2/3 pound chicken thigh, with or without skin
3 garlic cloves, peeled
1 inch fresh ginger, peeled
10 scallions (or more)
2 tablespoons oil
Handful dried red chiles (at least 10)
2/3 c roasted peanuts


Marinade:

1/2 teaspoon salt
2 teaspoons soy sauce
1 t shaoxing rice wine
1 1/2 t potato starch
1 tablespoon water


Sauce:

3 teaspoons sugar
3/4 t potato starch
1 teaspoon dark soy sauce
1 teaspoon soy sauce
3 t black chinese vinegar (Chinkiang)
1 teaspoon sesame oil
1 tablespoon chicken stock


Cut the chicken into 1/2 by 1/2 inch cubes. Place in bowl and mix in Marinade ingredients. Thinly slice the garlic and ginger. Chop scallions into pieces the same size as the chicken, keeping whites and greens separate. Snip the chiles in half and shake out seeds. Mix sauce ingredients.

Add 2 T oil to wok over high flame. When hot, but not smoking, add the chiles and Sichuan pepper; stir-fry briefly until oil is spicy and fragrant. Remove wok from heat and remove Sichuan pepper with a sieve.

Return to heat. Quickly add chicken and fry over a high flame, stirring constantly. As soon as chicken cubes separate, add ginger, garlic and white part of scallions. Continue cooking for a few minutes until meat is cooked through.

Stir the sauce and add it to the wok. Stir and toss. Once thick and shiny, stir in the peanuts. Serve hot witih plenty of rice.
