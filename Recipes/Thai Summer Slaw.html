Thai Summer Slaw


Light and refreshing, yet with punchy vibrancy and zing. I substituted savoy cabbage for the original napa cabbage with good success. For transporting to a picnic or potluck, put the slaw ingredients into a large sealable container and take the dressing along in a small bottle. Add the dressing to the slaw, reseal, and shake to combine to an hour before serving -- the salad stays nice and crispy.

Slaw:

2-3 cups thinly sliced napa cabbage or savoy cabbage
1/2 cup julienne-cut yellow squash (1/8-inch strips)
1/2 cup julienne-cut zucchini (1/8-inch strips)
1/2 cup julienne-cut red bell peppers (1/8-inch strips)
1/2 cup julienne-cut yellow bell peppers (1/8-inch strips)
1/2 cup julienne-cut, seeded, peeled cucumbers (1/8-inch strips)
1/2 cup shredded, peeled carrot
1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
1/4 cup thinly sliced green onions
1/4 cup grated radish
1 minced, seeded jalapeno pepper


Dressing:

3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
1 tablespoon fish sauce
1 tablespoon water
1 1/2 teaspoons sugar
1/2 teaspoon chile paste with garlic (such as sambal oelek)


In a large bowl, toss together all 11 Slaw ingredients.

Whisk the Dressing ingredients in a small bowl until the sugar has fully dissolved. Pour over the slaw and toss well to coat. Serve immediately.

(Yield, 10 servings about 1/2 cup each)
