Smoked Tongue


The first smoked tongue is in the picture with bacon (recipe not recorded)
The second version is a cured, cooked, then smoked version as described here. This was experimental...

Brine:

2 liters water
90 grams kosher salt
30 grams sugar
3 bay leaves
5 garlic cloves, smashed
2 teaspoons black peppercorns
1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
1 teaspoon coriander seeds
1 teaspoon yellow mustard seeds
30 grams pink salt (curing salt #1)


Bring all ingrediants to a boil. Simmer 4-5 minutes. Remove from heat. Cool to room temp, then cool in the fridge. Rinse and trim the tongue (apprx. 2.75 pounds starting weight). Remove a bit of the very think skin from the thick portion of tongue. Pierce meat all over with a sharp toothpick. Place meat in cooled brine. Chill for six days, turning every other day.

Remove meat from brine. Strain brine, keeping seasonings. Place seasonings, meat, one coarsely chopped onion, 2 carrots, 2 celery, 1.5 t k salt into pot and bring to a boil. Simmer 1 hour. Added 1.5 T additional k salt; simmered another 30 minutes. Peel skin while warm. Refridgerate overnight.

Smoke at 180-200 degrees with pecan. Cool, slice thinly.
