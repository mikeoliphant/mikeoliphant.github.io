Hand Pulled Noodles


The Dough:

200 g AP Flour
1/3 t k salt
140 ml water, room temp

Combine flour, salt in a bowl. Slowly add the water and mix with chopsticks until a sticky dough forms.
Knead with a little extra flour for about 10 minutes - until dough is smooth and springy.
Place dough in a bowl over a little more flour, then cover dough with a damp cloth, then cover bowl with plastic.
Let sit several hours.

The Noodles:

Cut dough into two pieces. Flatten and let rest 5-10 minutes. (Use extra flour as needed.)
Roll each piece into a rectangle about 1/4 inch thick. Let rest 30 minutes.
Cut each piece into long strips about 2 cm wide.

Bring a pot of water to a boil.
Pick up a strip, stretch it evenly and drop it into boiling water. Repeat once or twice.
Cook about one minute.
Remove noodles to a plate, drizzle with a few drops of sesame oil and mix by hand.
Repeat pulling and cooking until all dough is used.

Reheat as necessary: place in water 30 seconds to 2 minutes depending upon first cook level and whether or not noodles were refridgerated.
