Yucatecan Squid Salad




6 tablespoons freshly squeezed tangerine juice (satsuma)
2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
3 1/2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lime juice
1/2 shallot, finely diced
1/2 habanero chili, seeds removed
1/3 pound small squid, cleaned
1/2 cara cara orange, supremed and diced
1/4 haas avocado
1 teaspoon finely chopped fresh cilantro
Extra virgin olive oil
Freshly ground black pepper
Kosher salt


Rinse and drain the squid, then separate the bodies from the legs. Cut the bodies into 1/2-inch rings.

Prepare an ice bath and place near the stove top. Heat 2 cups of water to a boil, add 1 1/2 t salt. Add the squid bodies and simmer for 60 seconds, then add the squid legs and simmer another 60 seconds. Use a small strainer to lift the squid from the cooking liquid and immerse in the ice bath.

In a medium bowl, mix the citrus juices, habanero and shallot together. Drain the squid and add to the juices. Refrigerate for about 2 hours.

About 30 minutes before serving, discard the habanero and let the salad come to room temperature. Just before serving, add the chopped cilantro, orange and avocado pieces, a twist of black pepper and a generous drizzle of olive oil. Mix gently, taste and add salt if needed. Use a slotted spoon to serve.
