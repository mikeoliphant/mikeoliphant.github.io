Oyster Sauce Chicken


Lighter....

1/2 pound boneless skinless chicken breasts
1 tablespoon canola oil
3 pieces dried fungus
1/2 onion
2-3 bunches baby bok choy, rinsed well
1 tablespoon chicken broth
1/4 teaspoon salt


For the marinade, mix together in a medium bowl::

1 tablespoon oyster sauce
1 tablespoon dry sherry
2 teaspoons soy sauce
1 garlic clove, minced
1/2 teaspoon minced fresh ginger
1/4 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon sugar
2 teaspoons cornstarch


For the cooking sauce, mix together in a small bowl::

2 teaspoons soy sauce
2 tablespoons oyster sauce
1 tablespoon cornstarch
1/3 cup chicken broth


Soak the fungus in warm water for about half an hour.

Cut the chicken across the grain into 1/4 inch thick pieces, about 1 1/2 inch wide and an inch long. Add the chicken to the marinade and stir to coat. Add 1/2 teaspoon oil, stir, and set aside for about 15 minutes.

Slice the fungus into 1/4 inch strips, discarding the hard parts. Slice onion into half-inch strips. Slice the green top of the bok choy into half-inch strips crossways and do the same for the white bottom part, leaving a center piece of green and white about 3 inches long. Slice the center pieces longways into 2 or 3 half-inch strips. Put fungus, onion and bok choy into a bowl and set aside.

Heat a wok over high heat and add the 1 tablespoon of oil. When the oil shimmers, add the marinated chicken. Stir-fry until the meat is just cooked through (about a minute and a half). Remove chicken from the pan leaving excess oil behind.

Add the onions, fungus and bok choy. Stir-fry a minute, then add a tablespoon of water and 1/4 teaspoon of salt. Stir-fry 2-3 minutes longer, until the vegetables are crisp tender and all the cooked bits on the bottom of the pan have been incorporated into the sauce.

Return the chicken to the pan. Stir the cooking sauce and pour it into the pan. Cook, stirring until sauce bubbles and thickens, 1-2 minutes.

Serve over plain, short-grained rice.
