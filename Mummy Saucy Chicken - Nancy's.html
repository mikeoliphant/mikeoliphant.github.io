Mummy Saucy Chicken - Nancy's


The combination of lime juice, chilli and sugar gives this dish a delicious tangy, fresh taste.

1 (approx. 2 1/2 pound) small chicken
1 1/2 teaspoons salt
2 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce
1 teaspoon black pepper
4 limes
5 fresh red chiles (the long, skinny kind)
Approx. 1/4 cup oil
[sugar]


Cut the chicken into small pieces (ie: breasts in 3-4 pieces each, wings and thighs in half, legs). Leave the skin on.

In a large bowl, mix Â½ teaspoon salt, the soy and the black pepper. Toss the chicken pieces in the mixture and leave to marinade for 2 hours or more (you can do this in the morning, and let it marinade all day in the fridge).

Squeeze the juice from the limes.

Chop the chillies into quarters and blend them with the lime juice, a teaspoon of salt, and the sugar. If you do not have a blender, chop the chillies as well as possible, and mix them with the other ingredients.

Heat the oil in a frying pan and fry the chicken pieces until golden brown (about 15 minutes). Remove from the pan and transfer to a serving plate.

Pour the chili-lime mixture on top of the chicken and EAT!

NOTE: Beware of your fingers after dinner - the spiciness may linger...
