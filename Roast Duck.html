Roast Duck


Can also be done on the rotisserie. Ducks from 99 Ranch are whole: Trim off feet, neck and head before salting - save for poultry stock.

1 (5 pound) whole duck
1 tablespoon kosher salt
Fresh thyme sprigs
3-4 whole garlic cloves
3/4 cup water


Score the breast skin in a cross-hatch pattern, taking care not to cut into the meat. Sprinkle salt evenly inside and outside the duck, place on a rack over a pan and refrigerate, uncovered about 24 hours.
Prick the duck skin with a toothpick, put thyme and garlic into the cavity and let sit at room temperature for 45 minutes before cooking. Preheat the oven to 425f.
Put duck, breast up on a rack set over a pan of water (3/4 cup). Roast for for 30 minutes then carefully flip the duck and roast another 30 minutes. Carefully flip the duck breast side up and roast another 15 minutes or until done (~160f). Let rest at room temp 20 minutes before carving. Pour the pan dripping into a container, remove most of the fat and use drizzle the remaining jus over the bird at the table.
