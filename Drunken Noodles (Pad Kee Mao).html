Drunken Noodles (Pad Kee Mao)


If red thai chiles are not available, use a mixture of green thai chiles and red jalapeno chiles, or even all green thai chiles. Thai basil holds up better than italian basil, but either will work. Use at least one cup!

Sauce:

3 tablespoons oyster sauce
2 teaspoons seasoned soy sauce
1 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce
1 1/2 tablespoons black soy sauce (2x)
2 teaspoons sugar
1 tablespoon water
1 tablespoon fish sauce


Stirfry:

200 grams wide rice noodles (dry)
6 garlic cloves
5 red thai chiles
8 ounces meat (eg chicken thigh or pork shoulder)
Black pepper
1/2 onion, sliced long
3/4 red bell pepper, sliced long
6 small gai lan, cut into 1-inch pieces
4 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
1 cup basil leaves
Neutral oil


Boil the noodles for 5-6 minutes or until cooked, but not mushy. Drain and flush with cold water. Drain well and spread out on a baking sheet. Set aside.

Cut the meat into bite sized pieces. Season well with black pepper and set aside. Using a large mortar and pestle, smash the garlic and chiles into a paste.

Heat a wok on medium high heat. Add a couple tablespoons of oil. Add the garlic-chile paste, stir once or twice, then add the meat and stir well. Let the meat cook undisturbed until about half cooked. Add the onion, bell pepper and gai lan; stirfry for a minute or so. Add the noodles and sauce. Stir well and cook until sauce is absorbed and vegetables are crisp-tender (any where from 1 to 5 minutes). Stir the basil leaves through the noodles and serve immediately.
