Spanish Sausage


This sausage is sort of a take on Spanish Chorizo with plenty of paprika flavor.

The sausage meat is easier to grind and stuff into casings if kept quite cold. Over-working along with too much warmth softens the fat and can result in a spongy textured sausage.

2 1/2 pounds pork shoulder (with plenty of fat)
1 teaspoon fennel seeds
2 garlic cloves
1/3 cup dry white wine
1/4 cup sweet paprika
1 1/4 teaspoons half-sharp paprika
1 1/4 teaspoons cayenne pepper
3 teaspoons kosher salt
2 teaspoons freshly ground black pepper


Cut meat into finger-shaped strips. Grind coarsely along with the whole fennel seeds and garlic cloves. Chill 1 hour. Mix in the remaining seasonings, using a light hand to keep the meat texture from going mushy. Pinch off about a tablespoon of sausage and cook it in a small fry pan. Taste for seasoning - add additional chile or salt if required. Chill the sausage meat at least 2 hours or overnight before stuffing into hydrated and rinsed hog casings.
