Lamb Shanks with Ras el Hanout


Recipe calls for raisins -- we omitted them.

2 tablespoons olive oil
3 lamb shanks
2 onions, sliced
1 tablespoon "simple" ras el hanout (from Moroccan Cafe)
3/4 teaspoon paprika
1/2 teaspoon salt
2 cups meat stock
3/4 t beef boullion
1/2 teaspoon turmeric
1 t "simple" ras el hanout (from Moroccan Cafe)
Pinch cayenne pepper


Heat the oil and add the shanks - brown well and remove. Saute onions 'til soft with 1 T ras el hanout, paprika, and salt, then set aside.
Return shanks to the pan along with the meat stock, boullion, turmeric, 1 teaspoon ras el hanout, and cayenne. Cover and simmer about an hour. Return the onions to the pan, cover and simmer another 1 1/2 hours or until shanks are very tender. Remove shanks from pan, keep warm while reducing the broth to your desired consistancy. Skim excess fat from the sauce, season with salt and pepper to taste and serve together with the shanks.
