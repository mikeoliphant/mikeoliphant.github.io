Yams with Moroccan Compound Butter


I liked version 1 better, but I'm thinking that my measurements for version 1 are too high - especially since the cilantro is double version 2's but in the pictures it looks the same.

version 1 (2007):

1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 teaspoon olive oil
1/4 teaspoon ground cumin
1/4 teaspoon turmeric
1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
2 tablespoons chopped cilantro
1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice
1/4 cup butter, softened


Toast the cumin seeds in hot olive oil 30 seconds, then add the ginger and saute until fragrant and softened, about 2 minutes. Scrape into a small bowl and allow to cool completely. Mix everything thoroughly into the soften butter. Place onto a sheet of plastic wrap and shape into a log. Refrigerate until firm. Remove plastic wrap and slice into disks for serving.

version 2 (2008):

2 ounces (1/2 stick) unsalted butter, softened
1/4 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 teaspoon minced garlic (or 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger plus 1 teaspoon olive oil)
1/8 teaspoon turmeric
1/8 teaspoon ground cumin
1/8 teaspoon ground coriander
1/8 teaspoon cinnamon
1/16 teaspoon cayenne pepper
1 teaspoon fluffy lemon zest
1 tablespoon finely chopped cilantro
1/8 teaspoon kosher salt


Toast the cumin seeds in a dry, hot pan until fragrant, about a minute (OR if using ginger, toast the cumin seeds in hot olive oil 30 seconds, then add the ginger and saute until fragrant and softened, about 2 minutes.) Pour into a small bowl and allow to cool. Once the seeds have cooled, mix everything thoroughly into the soften butter. Place onto a sheet of plastic wrap and shape into a log by encasing the butter in the plastic and twisting the ends. Refrigerate until firm. Remove plastic wrap and slice into disks for serving.
