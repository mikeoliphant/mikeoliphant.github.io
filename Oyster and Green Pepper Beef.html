Oyster and Green Pepper Beef


Oyster sauce can be a really nice addition to almost any chinese dish ordinarily flavored with just soy sauce. If you prefer, broccoli can be substituted for the green peppers.

¾ to 1 pound boneless, lean beef
3 tablespoons canola oil
1 garlic clove, minced
1/2 teaspoon minced fresh ginger
1 onion, cut in 1/4-inch strips
2 small green peppers, cut in 1/4-inch strips
1 tablespoon water
1/4 teaspoon salt
3 dried shiitake mushrooms (sliced crimini mushrooms can be substituted)
3 pieces dried fungus


For the marinade, mix together in a medium bowl:

1 tablespoon oyster sauce
1 tablespoon soy sauce
1 tablespoon dry sherry
1/4 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon sugar
2 teaspoons cornstarch


For the cooking sauce, mix together in a small bowl:

1 tablespoon soy sauce
½ to 1 teaspoon asian chilli sauce
2 tablespoons oyster sauce
1 1/2 tablespoons cornstarch
1/2 cup chicken broth


Soak the mushrooms and fungus in warm water for about half an hour.

Cut the beef across the grain into thin strips, 1/8 inch thick by 1 ½ inch long, and about ½ inch wide.

Add the beef to the marinade and stir to coat. Add 1 1/2 teaspoons oil, stir, and set aside for about 15 minutes.

Slice the mushrooms and fungus into inch strips, discarding the hard parts.

Heat a wok over high heat and add 2 tablespoons of the oil. Add the garlic and ginger, then add the beef. Stir-fry until the meat is almost done, but still pink in the center (about a minute and a half). Remove the beef from the pan.

Heat the remaining tablespoon of oil in the wok. Add the onions, mushrooms, and fungus and stir-fry for 1 minute. Add the green pepper strips and cook for another half minute. Add a tablespoon of water and 1/4 teaspoon of salt, cover (a round pizza pan works well), and steam for a minute or two.

Return the beef to the pan. Stir the cooking sauce and add it to the pan. Cook, stirring until sauce bubbles and thickens.

Serve with plain, short-grained rice or over chinese egg noodles.
