Roast Tri-Tip with Wine Sauce




1 (2 pound) tri-tip roast
1 teaspoon salt
1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
2 tablespoons minced garlic
1 tablespoon minced, fresh rosemary


For the Wine Sauce:

1 cup beef broth
1/2 cup red wine
1 teaspoon thyme sprigs
2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
Cornstarch


In a small bowl, mix together the salt, pepper, thyme and rosemary. Rub the mixture all over the roast. Leave at room tempurature for 1 to 2 hours.

Roast at 450 degrees for about 25 minutes or until the internal temperature is 135-140 degrees. Remove from pan and let rest.

Place the pan over medium heat on the stove top. Add the red wine and use a wooden spoon to scrape the browned bits from the bottom of the pan. When most has dissolved, add the beef broth and thyme and bring to a boil. Reduce somewhat, then stir in the dijon. Make a slurry with cornstarch and a couple tablespoons of broth or water. Add enough slurry to the pan to thicken as desired.

Slice the meat into 3/8-inch slices and serve with the wine sauce.
