Paella


version 1: With shrimp, chicken, polish kielbasa and green beans.
version 2: see recipe
version 3 - mix and match ingredients!

1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
1 1/4 teaspoons kosher salt
2-5 oz Chorizo (Spanish, uncooked, Chistorra) or smoked sausage, sliced on the diagonal, 3/8 inch thick
about 1 pound chicken pieces, on the bone (2 legs, breast in 3-4 pieces) - optional
about 2/3 cup green beans in 2-inch lengths (handful)
about 2/3 cup red bell pepper in 1-inch diamonds (1/2 large, 3-4 oz)
about 2/3 cup green bell pepper in 1-inch diamonds (1/2 large, 3-4 oz)
1/2 cup onion in small dice (1 small)
zucchini in small dice
2 garlic cloves, minced
1 1/4 teaspoon smoked bittersweet paprika or regular paprika
1/2 cup chopped tomatoes
1/8 teaspoon saffron
2 3/4 cups chicken broth
1 1/4 cup rice (valencia)
4 ounces shrimp
clams


Put stock, saffron and salt in a pan and warm on the stove.

Heat pan to medium, add 2 T extra virgin olive oil. Brown slices of chorizo, then remove from pan and increase heat slightly. Put the chicken in the hot oil, skin side down; cook until golden brown on all sides; remove from pan.

Add 2 T oil, saute onion, bell peppers, zucchini and green beans; saute 2 minutes. Add garlic; after 30 seconds, add tomato and 1/2 t k salt. Stir and cook 2 minutes. Add paprika, stir for 30 seconds then add rice and stir to coat with oil.

Add seasoned chicken broth, stir. Return chicken and chorizo to pan; bring to a boil and simmer 10 minutes. (Until not soupy, but not dry)

Put into a 450 degree oven for ~14 minutes, adding shrimp at the 10 minute mark and clams at the 12 minute mark. Remove from oven, cover for 5 minutes. Put on hot burner for 2 minutes to create crispy bottom if desired.
